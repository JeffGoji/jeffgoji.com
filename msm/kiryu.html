Suspension Setup information

Ride height measurements were done at the pinch welds at the furthest ends of the front and back of the car on a 225/45/15 tire (23” diameter).
This method of ride height measurement will give you the most accurate and repeatable results and is a common standard in the Miata autocross and track world.
4.50 to 4.75 is a good range to work in, I run 0 rake, some drivers prefer some rake and some prefer negative rake.
In general on the NA/NB chassis you can adjust the rear ride height of the car by about 3/4ths of an inch before it begins to noticeably affect the alignment in any meaningful way.

I experimented with dozens of alignment settings and ultimately settled on the numbers below. I found that anything above 3.6 degrees of camber didn’t get enough heat in the front tires to make them work optimally, and anything above -2.8 degrees in the rear resulted in worse traction when launching and when trying to get power down out of a corner.

Swaybars are a pretty simple affair. I run a Racing Beat 1.25” front bar on it’s stiffest setting (inner-most hole) and the rear bar disconnected to let the helical gear differential hook-up on corner exit.

My car's history

This car has been amazing. Out of all the Miatas I can safely say the Mazdaspeed variants are probably my absolute favorite of the breed. You have that amazing NB2 chassis but you get a turbocharger and beefed up engine internals and drivetrain components, and did I mention the turbocharger?

In stock trim these cars got a dulled down treatment from Mazda with a reduced redline (a lowly 6700 rpm fuel cut) and boost pressure set to 8.5 psi by a factory electronic boost controller, all designed around making the engine last 200K miles and the drive train componnents just as long. Seems like a loser with a 2,527 lb curb weight, but what those of us who enjoyed modding our Miatas saw was potential for a 200 wheel-horsepower Miata with minimal investment and excellent reliability. And that is exactly what we got.

After winning my 2007 season in C-stock for the Houston region in a MSM I fell in love with the car. Finally a Miata that had power from the factory. Even though the turbocharger was handicapped and the redline was lower and the gearing in the 6-speed transmission made no sense, it still produced a whopping 154 horsepower and 144 lb-ft of torque at the wheels!
Which in case you haven't noticed is significantly more than the 115 horsepower and the 107 lb-ft of the regular 01-05 NB cars.
So in October 2008 I bought my own Mazdaspeed Miata. And I was quite happy for a very long time. In 2012 I began modifying it for a "anything goes" autocross class in the Houston BMW club and had a blast doing so.
First I added a FCM suspension with massive 800/500 springs and then 225/45/15 Autocross dedicated tires and a big 1 1/8th" Racing Beat front swaybar and a few other small tricks. The car was fun and it was fairly quick.
Over the next few years I modified and tweaked and eventually had a class-winning Mazdaspeed (2 time STO class champion in Houston region SCCA that would compete in the top 3 for top time of day on street tires from 2015 to 2018).
