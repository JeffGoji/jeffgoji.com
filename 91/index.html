1991 NA6 Mazda Miata



This is my 1991 MX5 that started it's life as a b-package with a factory hard top and factory viscous limited slip differential.I bought it used in February of 2003, and this car served as my entry into the Miata and Autocross world. Now typically I would write a review of a stock MX5 as that would be what the average buyer would be most likely to find. But the chances of finding an all-stock NA Miata 24 years after the last one rolled off the assembly line is to be honest, not very likely.
So instead I'm writing a review on my long term ownership experience with my own highly modified NA.

Miatas are not known for being powerful or quick or capable of winning races against minivans. Once you start adding power mods such as a full exhaust and intake and a shaved head, you still won't win. But it does make the engine "feel" more lively and will restore some of the missing ponies from those 20+ years of use. In the case of this particularly car, it dynoed a whopping 106 horses and 96lb-ft of torque at the wheels. Not fast by any means but it is fun.

At the time of writing this the youngest NA is 24 years old and the chassis was never a strong item on these cars when they were new and time and use only makes them more flexible and shudder prone.
There are a few really good options on the aftermarket to help restore some of that stiffness and reduce shudder over bumps.
