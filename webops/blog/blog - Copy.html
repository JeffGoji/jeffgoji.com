Blog Entry for 6/8/2021


As of yesterday I have officially enrolled in the Rice University Full-Stack Web Development program. And in doing so it puts my job search officially on hold for anything outside of a part-time job. Needless to say, I am very excited to be starting this program and I am looking forward to the journey and of course, the skills and job opportunities that await.

So you’re probably wondering why a self taught programmer would go the paid institution route, and that’s a damn fine question. The answer is that in this day and age, sadly, hiring managers want to see some kind of certificate to prove you can even advance to the interview level. And that’s not just me and my observations, that comes from talking to several hiring managers in the field two of whom are close friends.
College degree? Almost useless. It shows you knew how to party for 4 years.
Self-taught? Better have an amazing library of projects to back you up and a portfolio of websites ready to go (this takes a long time to build up on account of the fast and ready companies out there that want to make you site for an ultra low price and the “do it yourself” route that most customers choose to go down.)
Web Development is a huge field but it is also over-saturated and hiring managers need something more to get their attention.

Now the next question you might be asking is "Why Rice University?" Well, here in Houston, Rice University is a respectable institution and they have a proper “Career Development” service that works with you from day one to help you get a job. Not just another job board to post on and hope you land an interview or a job board that companies might post on and you’re hoping you get an interview in a tiny tank of fellow hungry sharks.

I think this is the best move I can make right now, and one that will lead to bigger and better things.
And if they do, wonderful! I’ll write about it here. If it does not, wonderful! I’ll write about it here.

Blog Entry for 5/24/2021


Today was a really good day. I spoke with a couple of recruiters, one of whom was really amazing and gave me some very valuable feedback and I like that she is a fellow Houstonian. I also had my first tech interview.
Naturally I hope something comes of these conversations on the job front, but if not I certainly hold no ill-will towards anyone I spoke to or the companies for not choosing me for a job or getting me hired somewhere.
Because if we look at it from another perspective, as people breaking into the tech industry we will spend many hours making coversheets and tweaking our resumes and hopefully interviewing and all with no success at all.
So we should enjoy the few really good experiences and learning opportunities we have during the process of trying to find that first developer job.
Like I always say, a day where you don't learn something is a day wasted.



Blog Entry for 5/23/2021


Hello world!
This will be the first of a series of entries for my blog on my path to becoming a Web Developer.
But first, a little bit about myself;
I learned HTML in 2000 and built a few websites for various things. I had a couple of fan sites for a couple TV shows I enjoyed, and I built several business pages for friends and for two customers. I never was a pro at JavaScript, but I knew enough to impliment shopping cart functions and features.

After a long career in Oil and Gas I decided to turn one of my hobbies and passions into my career and started coding again.
Catching up on all the latest technologies has been a lot of fun, and I am so thankful for Google and the wide array of databases with helpful information in them. I'll be posting a full list of resources other Junior Developers can utilize in a future Blog post, so stay tuned!

As it stands, I am completely caught up on the basics like HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, SQL, and the various tools (Bootstrap, jQuery, etc) to get what I want out of a page. I also taught myself Angular (which I am having a lot of fun with) and C# along with Python (I really like Python).
I also took some time to learn Word Press, and while I personally prefer actual coding from the ground up, I do understand its need in the world.
Going through and learning all these new methods has been a blast, and I am absolutely amazed at just how easy it is now to create apps, programs, websites, you name it.
The tools have come so far and really are amazing, and I am looking forward to a long career doing what I love.

- JAL.
